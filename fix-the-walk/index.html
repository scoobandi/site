The plan

Four weeks.
No wandering.

The sprint is paced on purpose: two lessons the first week, one lesson and a Q&A the second week, one lesson in week three, one lesson in week four, then a follow-through Q&A a month later.

Week 01 Two lessons to stop the chaos

Stop pulling now

Lesson one: do not bolt out the door, meet the treat chase game, and make the leash a game. Lesson two: build the “magic spot” while moving, standing, turning, and doing a 180.

Week 02 One lesson plus Q&A

Pay when we stop

Teach stay, introduce come, and bring your open questions plus resubmitted video to the mid-program Q&A.

Week 03 One lesson in the real world

Distractions without the meltdown

Add pressure. Find more “umph” when you need it. Learn the 1-2-3-4-5 of reactivity and what to do when a trigger appears.

Week 04 One lesson for the full walk

Put the pieces together

Make the walk easier, answer “do we need to punish?”, and teach a formal come while understanding why recall gets hard.

One month later Follow-through Q&A

Come back sharper

Bring the sticking points that only show up with time. We review, adjust, and keep progress moving.

Straight answers

Frequently asked questions.

Is this right for a seriously aggressive dog?

No. If your dog has done damage to a person or dog, you need deeper, individualized help. Start with a consult.

Do I have to attend live?

Live is best. If you cannot attend a lesson, watch the replay and keep pace within 48 hours. The video submissions and Q&A participation still matter.

What is the one game?

Every dog eats. Making food valuable is as close to a universal reward as we can get. Making a game out of that food makes the reward even more valuable and gives us a simple path to creating the patterns and behaviors we need to educate the dog. I literally use the same game to teach leash walking, come when called, stay, and all my other obedience. This game is simply called “treat chase.”

How much time will this take?

Outside of time in lessons with me, the recommended time requirement is 20 dedicated minutes with your dog every day. If you have time for twice a day, amazing. If you know starting out you have fewer than 5 days a week where you can spare time to train your dog, this is not the course for you. This is called a sprint for a reason.

What is Digital Dog School for?

Fix the Walk is the sprint. Digital Dog School is the backup library, included for extra help, review lessons, and support for the other dog problems that come up along the way.

What happens after the four weeks?

You keep support through the private community and return one month later for another Q&A to troubleshoot what practice has uncovered.
