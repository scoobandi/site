How the 4 weeks unfold

A focused sprint, not a six-month project.

Four weeks of structured live coaching, plus a follow-up Q&A one month later so the work sticks after real life gets loud again.

Week 1

Two lessons. Stop the pattern.

Stop leash pulling now — kindly. We start at the door and teach you how to gain and keep your dog's attention, then move right into teaching your dog how to walk on a leash by making it a game.

Week 2

One lesson + one live Q&A.

Getting it right pays well. Come, stay, and locking in the foundation. You upload videos of your dog and bring your questions to the live Q&A. Joel gives feedback on your specific dog, not a hypothetical one.

Week 3

One lesson. Layer in the skills.

Distractions, triggers, and what to do if you need a little more umph. This is where the walks start feeling different — and where you learn how to handle the moments that used to unravel you.

Week 4

One lesson. Take it into the real world.

Travel mode, a reliable recall, and getting rid of the problem for good. Real streets, real triggers, real reliability. You leave with a walk you can actually enjoy.

One month later A final follow-up Q&A so the progress holds after real life gets loud again. This is the difference between a 4-week bump and a 4-week transformation.

What the work actually looks like

Plan on 20 minutes a day, most days of the week. Some days will be a full session on your street. Some days will be a 5-minute drill in the driveway before work. Some days you'll just watch the week's lesson while you eat lunch.

If you can only give this 5 minutes a week, this isn't the right program. The dogs who transform in 4 weeks belong to owners who show up almost every day, even for a little.

The good news: your dog doesn't need a two-hour training session. Your dog needs you, consistently, for four weeks.
