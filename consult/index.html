Your dog may not have failed training. The plan may have failed your dog.

If you have already spent money and still feel stuck, you are not starting over from zero. You are bringing better information to the right kind of plan.

What happens on the call

This is not a vague "tell me about your dog" chat.

01

You tell me what is actually happening.

Not just the polished version. The walks, the bites, the fights, the anxiety, the guarding, the guests, the routines, the moments you are embarrassed to explain, and the parts of daily life that are starting to feel heavy.

02

I ask the questions that get underneath the behavior.

Most dog problems are not solved by guessing from the symptom. I want to understand the dog, the owner, the environment, the history, the pressure points, and the patterns that keep repeating.

03

We talk through what I would do if this were my dog.

You will get my honest read on what matters first, what I would stop doing, what I would build, what I would avoid, and how I would think about the path forward.

04

If it makes sense, we talk next steps.

Some dogs need private training. Some need Digital Dog School as part of the foundation. Some need Fix the Walk. Some need a bigger plan. If working together is the right move, we will talk about the program that actually fits your dog and your life.

The point of the consult is clarity. Before you spend more money, more time, or more emotional energy guessing, let's figure out what kind of help your dog actually needs.

Straight answers

Frequently asked questions.

Is this just a sales call?

No. The goal is to understand what is happening, help you make sense of it, and point you toward the right next step. If another option makes more sense, Joel will tell you.

What if I am embarrassed by my dog's behavior?

You do not need to perform or pretend. People usually book because things already feel emotional, messy, confusing, or hard to talk about.

What if my dog has bitten someone?

Start with a consult. Bite history, guarding, snapping, fighting, or unsafe household stress needs context before anyone gives you a plan.

What if I only need help with leash walking?

If leash walking is the main issue and you want a focused self-guided product, Fix the Walk may be the cleaner fit. If the walking problem is tied to aggression, panic, bite risk, or a bigger pattern, book the consult.

What if I am not sure whether I need DDS, Fix the Walk, or a consult?

That is exactly what the consult can help sort out. You can also take the quiz if you want a lighter first step.

Will you tell me exactly what program I need?

If working together makes sense, yes. The consult is where we sort out whether your dog needs private training, Digital Dog School, Fix the Walk, virtual coaching, or a bigger custom plan. I am not trying to shove every dog into the same offer. The whole point is to figure out what actually fits.

What if I have already wasted money on training?

That does not mean your dog is hopeless or that you failed. A lot of people come here after spending money on advice that did not fit their dog, their home, or their life. The consult helps us stop guessing and look at the whole picture before you spend more.

Is the consult useful if I am not ready to buy a bigger program?

Yes. You should still leave with a clearer understanding of what is going on, what matters first, and what kind of path makes sense. If ongoing help is the right move, we can talk about it. If another option fits better, I will tell you.

Will one consult fix everything?

No. A consult gives you clarity, direction, and next steps. Some dogs need a few sessions, some need a bigger plan, and some are better served by a course or focused program.

What happens after the consult?

If ongoing training, DDS, Fix the Walk, virtual coaching, or a bigger plan makes sense, Joel will tell you. If this is not a fit, that should be clear too.

Is this virtual or in person?

Both are possible. Appointments can happen in person in Sharpsburg, GA, or anywhere in the world through Zoom. Joel has successful 1-on-1 clients in 28 US states and 5 continents.
