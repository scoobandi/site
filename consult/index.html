How the consult works

Here's exactly what happens on the call

No sales pitch. No pressure to buy anything. Here's the 50 minutes, step by step:

1

You explain what's happening with your dog.

The behaviors, the history, what you've tried, what's making you crazy. I mostly listen.

2

I ask questions.

Specific ones. Because your dog isn't every dog, and I need to understand the actual pattern before I can tell you anything useful.

3

I tell you what I'd do.

Not in vague trainer-speak. The actual plan - what to work on first, what to stop doing, what the path looks like.

4

We talk about what success looks like for your household.

Not "your dog will be perfect." Real, specific outcomes that would change your life with this dog.

5

If we're a fit, I show you the options. If we're not, I tell you that too.

Some dogs need something I don't offer. Some owners aren't ready. Some cases need a vet behaviorist first. I'll be honest either way.

Before you book

The honest answers to what you're wondering.

How much does the consult cost?

It's free. 50 minutes with Joel. That's not a typo — the call is currently free, and it's a real diagnostic conversation, not a rushed sales pitch.

What if my case is really extreme?

That's exactly who this call is for. Aggression, bite history, dog fighting, panic — the cases other trainers won't touch are the ones Joel specializes in.

What if I'm not sure it's extreme enough?

Book anyway. If it's a better fit for Fix the Walk or Digital Dog School, Joel will tell you honestly. You don't have to earn your way onto this call.

Do you take international clients?

Yes. Joel regularly works with clients in Canada, the U.K., Australia, and across Europe. Virtual consults work beautifully — often better, because he sees your dog in your actual home.

Can I bring my dog to the in-person consult?

Yes — that's the point. The in-person visit includes a walk together so Joel can see your dog in real conditions. Proof of current vaccinations is required.

What if I already have another trainer?

Fine. Joel is not tribal about it. He'll evaluate your situation honestly and tell you what he'd do — whether that involves him or not.

Is there any obligation to book something after?

No. Book the consult, take the plan, and do what you want with it. If working together is the right move, you'll know — but there's no pressure.
